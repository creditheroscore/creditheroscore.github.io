Is Discover a Credit Card That Builds Credit?

  • Home
  • Blog
  • -Blog Details

In today’s financial landscape, building and maintaining good credit is more important than ever. Whether you’re applying for a mortgage, leasing a car, or even securing a job, your credit score plays a crucial role. One of the most common tools for building credit is a credit card—but not all cards are created equal. Among the many options available, Discover stands out as a popular choice for those looking to establish or improve their credit history.

But does Discover actually help build credit? Let’s dive deep into how Discover credit cards function, their benefits, and whether they’re the right choice for your financial journey.

How Credit Cards Help Build Credit

Before we focus on Discover specifically, it’s essential to understand how credit cards contribute to your credit score. The five major factors that influence your FICO score are:

  1. Payment History (35%) – Paying on time is the most critical factor.
  2. Credit Utilization (30%) – Keeping balances low relative to your credit limit.
  3. Length of Credit History (15%) – The longer your accounts are open, the better.
  4. Credit Mix (10%) – Having different types of credit (e.g., loans, credit cards).
  5. New Credit (10%) – Opening too many accounts in a short period can hurt your score.

A well-managed credit card can positively impact all these areas, making it a powerful tool for credit-building.

Discover’s Credit Card Options for Building Credit

Discover offers several credit cards, but not all are designed for those with limited or poor credit. Here are the primary options that can help build credit:

Discover it® Secured Credit Card

For those with no credit or bad credit, a secured credit card is often the best starting point. The Discover it® Secured Card requires a refundable security deposit (usually $200–$2,500), which becomes your credit limit.

Key Features:

  • Reports to All Three Major Bureaus – Discover reports to Experian, Equifax, and TransUnion, ensuring your responsible usage boosts your score.
  • Cashback Rewards – Unlike most secured cards, this one offers 2% cash back at gas stations and restaurants (up to $1,000 in purchases per quarter) and 1% on everything else.
  • Automatic Reviews for Upgrade – After eight months, Discover may review your account and transition you to an unsecured card, refunding your deposit.

Discover it® Student Cash Back

Students often have little to no credit history. The Discover it® Student Cash Back card is tailored for them, offering rewards while helping build credit.

Key Features:

  • No Credit History Required – Approval is based on income and potential rather than an extensive credit history.
  • Cashback Bonuses – 5% cash back on rotating categories (up to $1,500 per quarter) and 1% on everything else.
  • Good Grade Reward – A $20 statement credit each year for maintaining a GPA of 3.0 or higher.

Discover it® Chrome for Students

Similar to the Student Cash Back card but with a focus on gas and dining rewards.

Key Features:

  • 2% Cash Back at Gas Stations & Restaurants – Up to $1,000 in combined purchases each quarter.
  • No Annual Fee – A great perk for students on a budget.

Why Discover is a Strong Choice for Credit Building

1. No Annual Fees

Many credit-building cards charge annual fees, but Discover’s secured and student cards don’t. This makes them more accessible and cost-effective.

2. Credit Monitoring & Alerts

Discover provides free FICO® Score access and alerts for suspicious activity, helping users stay on top of their credit health.

3. Graduation to Unsecured Cards

Unlike some secured cards that trap users in a cycle of deposits, Discover actively reviews accounts for potential upgrades.

4. Rewards on a Credit-Building Card

Most secured and student cards don’t offer rewards, but Discover does—making it a rare and valuable option.

Potential Drawbacks

While Discover has many strengths, there are a few considerations:

  • Limited Acceptance Abroad – Discover isn’t as widely accepted internationally as Visa or Mastercard.
  • Lower Credit Limits – Secured cards start with your deposit amount, which may be limiting for some.

Final Thoughts

If you’re looking for a credit card that helps build credit while offering rewards and no annual fees, Discover is an excellent choice. Whether you opt for the Discover it® Secured Card or a student card, responsible use will steadily improve your credit score over time.

The key is to pay on time, keep balances low, and monitor your credit regularly. With Discover’s tools and benefits, you’ll be well on your way to a stronger financial future.
